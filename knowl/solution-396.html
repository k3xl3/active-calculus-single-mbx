Solution 6.4.5.3.1.

\(h\) ft above the bottom, a slice of thickness \(\Delta h\) has
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Volume }\approx 50\cdot 30\Delta h \hbox{ ft}{}^3. \end{equation*}
Since the density of water is \(\rho\) lb/ft\({}^3\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \hbox{Weight of the slice }\approx\rho(50 \cdot 30 \cdot \Delta h) \hbox{ lbs}. \end{equation*}
The distance to lift the slice of water at height \(h\) ft is \(8 - h\) ft, so the work to move one slice, \(\Delta W\text{,}\) is
\begin{equation*} \Delta W = \rho \cdot \hbox{Volume} \cdot \hbox{Distance lifted} \approx \rho(50 \cdot 30 \cdot \Delta h)(8 - h) = 1500 \rho (8 - h) \Delta h\hbox{ ft-lb}. \end{equation*}
The work done, \(W\text{,}\) to pump all the water is the sum of the work done on the pieces:
\begin{equation*} W \approx \Sigma\, 1500 \rho(8-h)\Delta h. \end{equation*}
As \(\Delta h \rightarrow 0\text{,}\) we obtain a definite integral. Since \(h\) varies from \(h = 0\) to \(h = 6\) and \(\rho=62.4\hbox{ lb/ft}{}^3\text{,}\) the total work is:
\begin{equation*} W = \int_0^{6} 93600 (8-h)dh = 93600 \left(8 h - {h^2\over 2}\right)\bigg|_0^{6} = 93600 (30) = 2808000\, \hbox{lbf}. \end{equation*}
