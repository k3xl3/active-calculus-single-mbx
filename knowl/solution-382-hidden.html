(a) Suppose we choose an \(x\text{,}\) \(0\leq x\leq 4\text{.}\) If \(\Delta x\) is a small fraction of a meter, then the density of the rod is approximately \(\delta(x)\) anywhere from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters from the left end of the rod. The mass of the rod from \(x\) to \(x+\Delta x\) meters is therefore approximately \(\delta(x) \Delta x = (5 + 2 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) If we slice the rod into \(N\) pieces, then a Riemann sum is \(\Sigma_{i=1}^{N}(5 + 2 x_i)\Delta x\text{,}\) or, more loosely, \(\Sigma (5 + 2 x)\Delta x\text{.}\) (b) The definite integral is
\begin{equation*} M=\int_0^{4} \delta(x)\,dx = \int_0^{4} (5 + 2 x)\,dx= (5 x + 1 x^2)\bigg|_0^{4} = 36 \hbox{ grams}. \end{equation*}
