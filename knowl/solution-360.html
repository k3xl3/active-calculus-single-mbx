Solution 6.1.5.2.1.

Over the interval \([0,\pi]\text{,}\) the graphs \(y = 8\sin x\) and \(y = 9 \cos x\) intersect when \(8\sin x = 9 \cos x\text{,}\) i.e., \(\tan x = \frac{9}{8}\text{,}\) i.e., \(x=\tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{8})=0.844153986113171\text{.}\) Let \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{8})\text{.}\) Over the interval \(\left[0, t\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 8\sin x\) lies below that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-8 \sin x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =\left.(9\sin x+8\cos x)\right|_0^{ t} \cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+8\cos t\right)-(9\cdot 0+8\cdot 1)\cr \amp =\left(9\sin t+8\cos t\right)-8 \end{array} \end{equation*}
Since \(t = \tan^{-1}(\frac{9}{8})\text{,}\) we have \(\tan t = \frac{9}{8}\text{.}\) Considering a rightangle triangle with an angle which opposite side has length 9 and adjacent side has length 8, we see that the hypotenues is \(\sqrt{145}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sin t = \frac{9}{\sqrt{145}}\) and \(\cos t = \frac{8}{\sqrt{145}}\text{.}\) \(9\sin t+8\cos t = \frac{9^2}{\sqrt{145}} + \frac{8^2}{\sqrt{145}} = \frac{9^2+8^2}{\sqrt{145}} = \frac{145}{\sqrt{145}}=\sqrt{145}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\int_0^{ t}(9 \cos x-8 \sin x)dx= \sqrt{145}-8.\) Over the interval \(\left[ t,\pi\right]\text{,}\) the graph of \(y = 8\sin x\) lies above that of \(y = 9 \cos x\text{.}\) Hence, the area between the two curves is \(\int^\pi_{ t}(8\sin x - 9 \cos x)dx\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} \amp =-\left.(9\sin x+8\cos x)\right|^\pi_{ t} \cr \amp =8 + \sqrt{145} \end{array} \end{equation*}
Thus the total area is the sum of the above two integrals, which is \(2\sqrt{145}\text{.}\)
