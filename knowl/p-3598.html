There is always a critical number of \(p\) at \(x = 0\text{.}\) But the other critical number at \(x = \frac{2a}{3}\) and the inflection point at \(x = \frac{a}{3}\) both obviously depend directly on \(a\text{:}\) as we increase the value of \(a\text{,}\) both the location of the critical number and the inflection point move to the right along with \(a\text{.}\)
