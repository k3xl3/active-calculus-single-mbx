By the constant multiple rule, \(p'(r) = 4\frac{d}{dr}[\sqrt{r^6 + 2e^r}]\text{.}\) Using the chain rule to complete the remaining derivative, we see that

\begin{equation*} p'(r) = 4 \frac{1}{2\sqrt{r^6 + 2e^r}} \frac{d}{dr}[r^6 + 2e^r] = \frac{4(6r^5 + 2e^r)}{2\sqrt{r^6 + 2e^r}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
