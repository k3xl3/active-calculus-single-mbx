\(f(x)=\ln(x/4)\) and \(g(x)=x^2-16\text{,}\) so \(f(4)=0\) and \(g(4)=0\) and l'Hopital's rule can be used. To apply l'Hopital's rule, we first find \(f'(x)=1/x\) and \(g'(x)=2x\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x \rightarrow 4} \frac{\ln(x/4)}{x^2-16} = \lim_{x \rightarrow 4} \frac{1/x}{2x}=\lim_{x \rightarrow 4} \frac{1}{2x^2}=\frac{1}{32}. \end{equation*}
