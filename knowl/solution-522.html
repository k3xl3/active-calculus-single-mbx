Solution 8.5.6.3.1.

\(\displaystyle \frac{1}{x}\) and its first three derivatives are \(f(x) = \displaystyle \frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) \(f'(x) = -{1\over x^2}\text{,}\) \(f''(x) = {2\over x^3}\text{,}\) and \(f'''(x) = -{6\over x^4}\text{.}\) Thus, evaluating these at \(x = 1\text{,}\) we get the terms term 0 = \({1}\) term 1 = \(-{1}(x - 1)\) term 2 = \({1}(x - 1)^2\text{,}\) and term 3 = \(-{1}(x - 1)^3\text{.}\) Thus the series is
\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \frac{1}{x} = {1} - {1}(x - 1) + {1}(x - 1)^2 - {1}(x - 1)^3 + \cdots. \end{equation*}
