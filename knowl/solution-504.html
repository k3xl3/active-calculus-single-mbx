Solution 8.4.6.1.1.

(a) For \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} {(-1)^n\over 3 n}\text{,}\) we have \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} {1\over 3 n} = 0\text{.}\) Thus, by the alternating series test, we know that this series converges (it is at least conditionally convergent). Because \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} {1\over 3 n}\) diverges (because it is a constant times a \(p\)-series with \(p=1\)), is only conditionally, not absolutely, convergent. (b) For \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} {(-1)^n\over 3^n}\text{,}\) we have \(\lim\limits_{n\to\infty} {1\over 3^n} = 0\text{.}\) Thus, by the alternating series test, we know that this series converges (it is at least conditionally convergent). Because \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty} {1\over 3^n}\) also converges (by comparison to a \(p\)-series or by the integral test), our original series must be not only conditionally convergent but absolutely convergent.
