\(F'(x)=e^{-x^2/5}\) and \(F(0)=3\text{,}\) we have
\begin{equation*} F(x)=F(0)+\int_0^x e^{-t^2/5}\,dt = 3 + \int_0^x e^{-t^2/5}\,dt. \end{equation*}
Substituting \(x=4\) and evaluating the integral numerically gives
\begin{equation*} F(4)=3 + \int_0^{4} e^{-t^2/5}\,dt \approx 4.95905. \end{equation*}
