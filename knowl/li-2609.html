Item 4.1.5.7.b.

In part (a), we established that \(s(1) = 12\text{,}\) so the biker traveled \(11\) miles north in the first hour. For the second hour, the biker travels due south at \(10\) mph, and thus travels \(10\) miles south. This means that at \(t=2\text{,}\) their position is \(s(2) = s(1) - 10 = 12 - 10 = 2\) We were given that \(s(0) = 1\text{.}\) Thus, \(s(2) - s(0) = 1\text{,}\) and the biker is \(1\) mile north of the lake.
