Multiplying by the least common denominator gives

\begin{equation*} -\left(4x^{2}+x+32\right) = A(x^2+9)+(Bx+C)(x - 4) \end{equation*}

Rearranging terms on the right hand side, yields

\begin{equation*} -\left(4x^{2}+x+32\right) = (A+B)x^2+(-4B+C)x + 9 A - 4C \end{equation*}

Now we equate the coefficients:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rl} A+B \amp = -4 \\ -4B +C \amp = -1 \\ 9 A - 4C \amp = -32 \end{array} \end{equation*}

Solving the system gives \(A=-4, \; B = 0\) and \(C= -1\) so the partial fraction decomposition is

\begin{equation*} \frac{-\left(4x^{2}+x+32\right)}{(x - 4)(x^2+9)} = \frac{-4}{x - 4} + \frac{-1}{x^2+9} \end{equation*}

The definite integral is then

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle \int \frac{-\left(4x^{2}+x+32\right)}{(x - 4)(x^2+9)} dx = -4\ln\!\left(\left|x-4\right|\right) + \frac{-1\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{x}{3}\right)}{3} +C \end{equation*}
