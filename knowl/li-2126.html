Item 3.1.4.6.b.

Given that \(h\) is decreasing for \(x \lt 0\) and increasing for \(x \gt 0\text{,}\) the function \(h\) has an absolute minimum value at \(x=0\text{.}\) So one root is to the left of this minimum and the other to the right. Thus, one root is negative and the other positive. See, for instance, the lowest of the three possibilities for \(h\) shown in the figure in (a).
