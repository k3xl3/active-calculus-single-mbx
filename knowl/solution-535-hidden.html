\(\displaystyle \frac{5}{1-6 x} = 5 \cdot \sum_{n=0}^\infty (6 x)^n\text{.}\) Then we can see that \(c_n = 5 \cdot 6^n\text{,}\) making \(c_0 = 5\text{,}\) \(c_1 = 5 \cdot 6 = 30\text{,}\) \(c_2 = 5 \cdot 6^2 = 180\text{,}\) etc. Regarding the radius of convergence, we perform the standard ratio test:
\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| \frac{5(6 x)^{n+1}}{5(6 x)^n} \right| = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left| 6 x \right| = \left| 6 x \right| \end{equation*}
And then \(\left| 6 x \right| \lt 1 \iff \left| x \right| \lt \frac 1 {6}\text{,}\) making \(R = \frac 1 {6}\text{.}\)
