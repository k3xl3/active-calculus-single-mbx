Because \(a_n \approx r^n\) for large \(n\text{,}\) this means that the series \(\sum a_n\) acts like the series \(\sum r^n\) for sufficiently large \(n\text{.}\) The latter is a geometric series with ratio \(r\) between successive terms. Said differently, \(\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \approx \frac{r^{n+1}}{r^n} = r\text{.}\)
