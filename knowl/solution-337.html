Solution 5.5.5.1.1.

\begin{equation*} \frac{2x}{x^2-36}=\frac{A}{x+6}+\frac{B}{x-6} \end{equation*}
so that
\begin{equation*} 2x = A(x-6)+B(x+6) = (A+B)x + 6 (-A+B). \end{equation*}
Solving, we get \(A=B=1\text{,}\) so that
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-36}\,dx= \int \left(\frac{1}{x+6}+\frac{1}{x-6}\right)\,dx =\ln|x+6|+\ln|x-6|+C. \end{equation*}
Using the substitution \(w=x^2-36\text{,}\) we get \(dw= 2x\,dx\text{,}\) so we have
\begin{equation*} \int \frac{2x}{x^2-36}\,dx= \int \frac{dw}{w}=\ln|w|+C = \ln|x^2-36|+C. \end{equation*}
The properties of logarithms show that the two results are the same:
\begin{equation*} \ln|x+6|+\ln|x-6| = \ln|(x+6)(x-6)| =\ln|x^2-36|. \end{equation*}
