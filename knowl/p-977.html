Since \(F\) is measured in degrees Fahrenheit and \(t\) in minutes, we have \(F'(10) \approx -3.33592\) degrees F per minute. This means that at the instant \(t=10\text{,}\) the temperature of the coffee is decreasing at an instantaneous rate of about \(3.33592\) degrees per minute. Said differently, in the next minute, we'd expect the temperature of the coffee to drop about \(3.33592\) degrees.
