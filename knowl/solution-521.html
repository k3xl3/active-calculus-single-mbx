Solution 8.5.6.2.1.

\begin{equation*} g(x) = g(8) + g'(8)(x-8) + \frac{g''(8)}{2!}(x-8)^2 +\frac{g'''(8)}{3!}(x-8)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
Substituting gives
\begin{equation*} g(x) = 4 + 1(x-8) + \frac{4}{2!}(x-8)^2 +\frac{5}{3!}(x-8)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
(a) The degree 2 Taylor polynomial, \(P_2 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-8)^2\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_2(x) = 4+1\!\left(x-8\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(x-8\right)^{2}. \end{equation*}
(b) The degree 3 Taylor polynomial, \(P_3 (x)\text{,}\) is obtained by truncating after the \((x-8)^3\) term:
\begin{equation*} P_3 (x) = 4+1\!\left(x-8\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(x-8\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 5\!\left(x-8\right)^{3} \end{equation*}
(c) Substitute \(x=8.1\) into the Taylor polynomial of degree 2:
\begin{equation*} P_2(4.9) = 4+1\!\left(8.1-8\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(8.1-8\right)^{2} = 4.12. \end{equation*}
From the Taylor polynomial of degree 3, we obtain
\begin{equation*} P_3(4.9) = 4+1\!\left(8.1-8\right)+\frac{1}{2!}\cdot 4\!\left(8.1-8\right)^{2}+\frac{1}{3!}\cdot 5\!\left(8.1-8\right)^{3} = 4.12083. \end{equation*}
